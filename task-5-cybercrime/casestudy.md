## Case Study: The "Urgent Update" Ransomware Attack 

**Ransomware** is a type of malware that encrypts a victim’s files, making them inaccessible until a 
ransom—usually in cryptocurrency—is paid to the attacker. It essentially turns digital data into a 
hostage. 

### The Scenario: City of "Riverview" Municipal Breach 

In this realistic scenario, a mid-sized municipal government, the City of Riverview, falls victim to a 
sophisticated ransomware strain known as *LockBit*. 

**Step-by-Step Execution:** 


1.  **Infiltration:** An employee in the HR department receives an email appearing to be from alegitimate software vendor, claiming an "urgent security patch" must be downloaded via a link. 
2.  **Deployment:** Once the employee clicks the link and runs the file, the malware enters the 
network. It quietly spends 48 hours moving laterally through the system to identify the city’s primary 
database and backup servers. 
3.  **Encryption:** At 2:00 AM on a Sunday, the ransomware activates, encrypting police records, 
utility billing systems, and public works schedules. 
4.  **The Demand:** On Monday morning, staff find "README" text files on every screen 
demanding **$500,000** in Bitcoin for the decryption key. 

### Targets and Consequences

Ransomware attackers typically target, **"resource-rich but security-poor"** entities, such as local 
governments, hospitals, and schools, where downtime has immediate, high-stakes consequences. 

The impact on Riverview is devastating. Beyond the financial demand, the city faces **massive 
operational paralysis**: residents cannot pay water bills, and emergency services must revert to 
manual paper logging. Even if the ransom is paid, there is no guarantee the data will be recovered, 
and the city faces long-term **reputational damage** and high recovery costs.
